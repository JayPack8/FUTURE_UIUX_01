### **Target Users**



The primary users are young online shoppers (ages 18–30) who discover products through social media advertisements. These users make quick decisions and prioritize trust, clear product information, and fast delivery.



Secondary users include returning customers who browse categories and compare multiple products before making a purchase.





### **Layout Structure**



The website is structured to support fast product discovery and decision-making:



**Homepage**: Designed as a shopping hub with category shortcuts, trending products, and current deals to guide users quickly.



**Product Cards**: Compact and scannable, highlighting key information such as product image, price, and essential specs.



**Product Page**: Treated as the primary entry point, with a strong focus on visuals, pricing, delivery information, and trust indicators.



**Trust Section**: Positioned to reassure users with key messages such as verified products, fast delivery, secure payment, and easy returns.



**Footer**: Provides support links, contact access, and trust statements to reinforce credibility.





### **UX Decisions**



Prioritized product pages since most users arrive from ads, ensuring immediate clarity and trust.



Designed a clear visual hierarchy (image → price → product name) to support fast scanning.



Limited category exposure on the homepage to avoid overwhelming users and improve navigation.



Used consistent spacing, typography, and card structures to maintain a clean and modern interface.



Implemented visible trust signals (delivery info, secure payment, returns) to reduce hesitation.



Simplified forms and interactions to reduce friction and improve usability.



### **Conversion Improvements**



The redesign improves conversions by:



Enabling users to quickly evaluate products through clear visuals and concise information.



Increasing trust with visible guarantees and structured product details.



Reducing decision time through simplified layouts and focused content.



Improving navigation and discoverability, allowing users to find relevant products faster.



Minimizing friction in key interactions such as browsing, selecting, and contacting support.
